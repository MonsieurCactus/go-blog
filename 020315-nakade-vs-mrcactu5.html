## Playing Go
## Hello World! For this first blog entry, well discuss a 0.5 kome game I played agained 2k `nakade11` Here, exactly 100 moves in, the game looks favorable to Black except there for some questions about the life or death status on the upper left. The upper right being quite clear. The bottom half is just begginning to be filled, with a crosscut at **K4**
## Solve it! The best source of Go problems is from the game itself. I spent the entire game thinking about the upper left and whether it as alive. **Is Black dead?** As you are reading the rest of the blog this is something to play with. Until I write a decent online Go board, you will have to use the real thing.
Familiar and unexciting openings for the first ten moves. Then White seems to want both the top side and break up my area on the left. It worked out for him, because I couldn't not pick the right *joseki* opening. Here, I do the low approach and he response with a **two-space** pincer. **O** marks the move I should I have played instead of **C17**.
Rui Naiwei's joseki book starts with the low approach to 3-4. She admires its flexibility, casually letting oppening play one, two or even three moves. **Does this opening look good for white?** This opening is taken from [fuseki.info](http://www.fuseki.info/). White seems to have the choice as to whether I take the corner or not. Here is the alternative...
50 moves into the game, my incorrect joseki fossilizes. The remaining star points, **K4** and **Q10** look attractive comparsed on any of the fighting we have played so far...
**How to attack the empty space?** **Find the best move** Black... if not completely settled, has room to escape. White on the other hand not so much. Is it advantageous for Black to try?
